BCIJelly: Open-Source BCI Framework

Addressing critical challenges in the BCI field such as data fragmentation, high development barriers, and application bottlenecks, Prof. Tielin Zhang's team at CEBSIT (CAS) introduces BCIJelly—an integrated intelligent ecosystem providing a comprehensive 'Data-Model-System' solution.

Data Layer: Integrates multi-species neural signals (human, monkey, mouse) with standardized processing pipelines and unified data interfaces.

Algorithm Layer: Aggregates classic and cutting-edge decoding models. Innovatively introduces the BJI_Agent, which automatically searches and combines optimal model architectures, significantly lowering the barrier to algorithm development.

System Layer: Provides data management, model invocation, visual analysis tools, an open-source Python library, and online Web services to support scientific collaboration and rapid validation.

Furthermore, BCIJelly enables efficient deployment on FPGAs and neuromorphic chips, propelling BCI technology from laboratory research to real-world applications and establishing vital support for field standardization.

TEAM
INTRODUCTION

The technical team originates from Prof. Tielin Zhang's research group at the Center for Excellence in Brain Science and Intelligence Technology (CEBSIT), Chinese Academy of Sciences.

The group is dedicated to deeply drawing inspiration from brain atlas structures, functional activities, and plasticity mechanisms to develop biologically plausible and computationally efficient brain-inspired neural network models.
